I saw an interesting idea for an AI housekeeper. It felt more concrete—and more valuable as a product—than the usual vague “personal assistant.”

One point was that an open-source model could run locally in a lightweight form and still respond quickly.

I suddenly understood something Yubo, the founder of YouMind, had said before: the strongest model is not always the best choice; what matters is whether it fits the current task.

I used to give everything the best model I could access. A personal housekeeper may need something different:

- access to approved personal information;
- consistent preferences and ways of working over time;
- local or otherwise controllable execution;
- being good enough for one person’s tasks rather than first on every benchmark.

Everyone imagines an AI housekeeper as a secretary. The more mature part of this idea is grounding it in local models, personal customisation, and specific workflows.
