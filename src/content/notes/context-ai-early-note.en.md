Reading this now, I am glad I chose to study my bachelor’s degree in Australia.

The environment is diverse and it does train you, although the differences between courses and classmates can be large. I slowly developed the ability to read papers while preparing presentations last semester.

Honestly, a lot of the strongest papers still need to be read in English, including material on the ACM website. Keep learning. I was planning to try Claude more.

The idea of contextual AI felt interesting and new—another step beyond the reasoning pattern. I was looking forward to seeing the finished products.

> To some extent, your English level shapes the limits of what you can access and understand.
