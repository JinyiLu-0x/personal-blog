It suddenly made me wonder: product roles do competitor analysis, so does this count as something adjacent? I do not really understand product roles yet. Leaving it here because it feels useful.

> “Internally, growth and monetisation are treated as the same thing.”

I did not understand that sentence at first, so I threw it to GPT for a detailed explanation.

DY did not grow simply by throwing money at user acquisition. Its growth and monetisation worked unusually well together, which meant it could “buy users” more efficiently than others.

If acquiring one user costs 10, a platform with strong recommendation and retention might earn 15 from that user. If Slow Foot only breaks even or keeps a tiny margin, it cannot afford the same acquisition cost. The winner is not simply whoever is willing to spend more. The users still need to stay and keep creating value.

That angle makes sense to me. I used to hear “the algorithm is strong” and only think the experience was better. Now I see how directly it can land in growth and monetisation.
