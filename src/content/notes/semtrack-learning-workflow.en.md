Course requirements and grade calculations were scattered everywhere, and explanations from teachers and tutors were fragmented. Simply clarifying requirements, timelines, and calculations took a lot of time.

So I spent a few hours vibe-building a Todo List website exactly around my preferences. The main stack was React; nothing especially complicated.

The first version was frontend-only, with data in browser local storage. Records remained after closing the page unless the browser cache was cleared. I left the backend for later and focused on solving the immediate problem.

This project shows how I use technology to improve a workflow:

1. organise dates, requirements, and dependencies from materials I am allowed to access;
2. turn repeated checking and manual calculation into a structured interface;
3. use AI to accelerate prototyping, layout, and debugging;
4. verify against original course material and decide what is recorded.

The demo felt smooth enough to publish. Its main value is showing how I turn a real problem into a product and keep improving my workflow. It does not do coursework for someone; it reduces the time spent reorganising and searching for course information.
