I found a potentially useful way to think about attention management, so I am leaving it here.

Distraction may not be only “too much information.” It can be three problems stacked together: information overload, execution drift, and a lack of feedback.

AI is reasonably suited to:

- finding what is actually relevant in a pile of material;
- shrinking a goal into the next executable action;
- placing feedback points in the process;
- reviewing which steps repeatedly fail to happen.

But I do not agree with exaggerated claims about handing all time management over to AI. It can help design records, reminders, and reviews. The goals, priorities, and life decisions still belong to the person.
