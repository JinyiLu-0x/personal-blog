Driving practice during the day, development at night. The result of two days: a very rough Prototype 1.0.

At the time I still had nearly twenty unimplemented ideas and kept adding whatever feature came to mind. A few icons looked awkward too. The UI borrowed from a lot of software I already liked.

But the core was there: AI-assisted coding on the left, LinkVerse’s knowledge graph and workspace on the right. Nodes, edges, deletion, dragging, and state synchronisation were becoming real interactions rather than a static mockup.

Of course it was not a mature product. Make the idea usable first, then gradually narrow the scope.

The full project record is here: [LinkVerse Knowledge Workspace](/en/projects/linkverse).
